Keywords: PhD Secondment, University of Waterloo, Canada.

In **2019**, I completed a secondment at the **University of Waterloo** in Canada. This stay provided a unique chance to collaborate with researchers, experience a different academic culture, and connect my PhD work with international expertise.

![University of Waterloo collaboration 1](/images/timeline/UoW1.jfif)

## Focus of the secondment

During my time at Waterloo, I:

- Worked on **research topics closely related to my PhD**, deepening both the theoretical understanding and practical relevance
- Engaged in **technical discussions and group meetings**, which offered new perspectives on familiar problems
- Learned about **local research priorities and industrial collaborations** in Canada

These activities helped me refine my own research questions and identify directions for future work.

![University of Waterloo collaboration 2](/images/timeline/UoW2.jfif)

## Experience and impact

Beyond the research, the secondment was an important personal and professional experience:

- Adapting to a **new academic environment and culture**
- Building an **international network** of colleagues and collaborators
- Gaining confidence in presenting and defending ideas in a different setting

Overall, the secondment to the University of Waterloo strengthened both my PhD project and my broader outlook as a researcher.
