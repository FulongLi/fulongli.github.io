After completing my **PhD**, I joined **Loughborough University** as a **Research Associate**, continuing my research on **DC microgrids** under an **EPSRC**-funded project. The work here builds directly on my doctoral research, with a stronger focus on **control strategies** for low-voltage DC microgrid systems.

## Hierarchical Control Strategy

The core of my research at Loughborough revolves around a **hierarchical control framework** for DC microgrids, which is structured into three layers:

- **Layer 1 — Converter-Level Control:** The lowest layer deals with the direct control of individual power converters, ensuring stable voltage and current regulation at the device level.
- **Layer 2 — Multi-Converter Coordination:** The middle layer addresses the coordination and control of multiple converters operating in cascade or in parallel, managing their interactions and ensuring seamless cooperation.
- **Layer 3 — System-Level Power Flow Management:** The highest layer focuses on power flow control and optimisation across the entire microgrid, balancing generation modules and loads to achieve efficient and reliable operation.

## Building a Low-Voltage DC Microgrid Testbed

A significant part of my work at Loughborough involves **designing and building a low-voltage DC microgrid experimental platform**. This testbed serves as a physical environment for validating the proposed hierarchical control strategies and exploring practical challenges that arise in real hardware.
