Keywords: Industrial Secondment, DC Systems BV, The Netherlands, DC protection, DC microgrid.

In the **summer of 2017**, I completed a secondment at **DC Systems BV** in the Netherlands. At the time, DC Systems BV was a startup focused on DC power systems—it has since been acquired by **Schneider Electric**. During my visit, I was engaged with their work on **DC protection** and their research and products related to **DC microgrid** technology.

<!-- Add image: Netherlands secondment photo -->
![Secondment at DC Systems BV, Netherlands](/images/timeline/DCBV2017.jpg)

## What I worked on

- **DC protection**—development and testing of protection solutions for DC systems
- **DC microgrid**—research and product development
- **Real-world applications**—I had the chance to visit several installations where DC microgrids were in use. I remember one application in particular: a DC system used in a **plant-growing facility** (such as a greenhouse), which showed how these technologies are applied in practice.

## Life in the Netherlands

I lived in a **shared house** during my stay, and it remains one of my best memories. The Netherlands is a beautiful country, and the place I stayed was near **Schiphol Airport**, close to Amsterdam—making it easy to explore the region.

That summer was one of the most memorable periods of my life. I am grateful to have had this experience and to expose myself to an **industrial environment** early in my career. It has had a lasting positive impact on how I think about both industry and academia, and how I approach the bridge between research and real-world applications.
