In **2017**, I had the opportunity to complete a secondment at **DC Systems BV** in the Netherlands. This experience combined hands-on engineering exposure with a valuable exchange between academic research and industrial practice.

## What I worked on

During the secondment, I focused on understanding real-world constraints and workflows, including:

- **System-level requirements** and how they shape design decisions
- **Implementation and testing practices** used in industry
- **Technical discussions** that helped connect research ideas to practical needs

## What I learned

Beyond the technical aspects, the secondment strengthened skills that are essential for impactful engineering:

- Turning complex problems into **clear specifications**
- Communicating trade-offs with **non-academic stakeholders**
- Prioritizing **robustness, safety, and maintainability**

## Looking back

I’m grateful for the chance to work with the team at DC Systems BV and for the insights gained from seeing engineering challenges from a different perspective. The secondment helped me think more practically about how research can translate into products and systems that work reliably in the field.
